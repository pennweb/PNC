The December 1998 plenary meeting of the Penn National Commission on Society, Culture and Community was entitled "Enriching the Conversation: Community in the 21st Century"

On Thursday, December 17th, the meetings took place at the architecturally significant Getty Center. Among the sessions that day were a discussion of the cultural context of public discourse, the conduct of public discourse, and concurrent sessions on the role of key institutions -- universities, museums, and foundations -- in leading civic discourse. Participants for the sessions on Thursday included author Jonathan Franzen, author Samuel Popkin, Edward Rothstein, cultural critic for the New York Times, Professor Richard Weisberg, Cardozo School of Law, Yeshiva University, Dr. Barry Munitz, President of the Getty Trust.

On Friday the 18th, the Commission turned its attention to building discourse and community outside of the U.S. with examinations of recent developments in South Africa, Northern Ireland, Israel, and Eastern Europe featuring a presentation by Mr. Alex Boraine, Vice Chair of South Africa's Truth and Reconciliation Commission. Also participating were Mr. Robin Wilson of Northern Ireland's Democratic Dialogue, and Professor G.M. Tamas, the E.L.Wiegand Distiguished Visiting Professor at Georgetown University.

Portions of the presentations and discussions were webcast live from the PNC website, and are presently available online.

Transcripts of the meeting proceedings are available online.
