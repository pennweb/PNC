The sixth and concluding plenary session of the Penn National Commission's first three years took place November 8-9, 1999, when Commission members returned to the University of Pennsylvania campus in Philadelphia to discuss Celebrating the Conversation: Public Discourse in Action This meeting reviewed the effectiveness of contemporary discourse programs and considered ways to improve the actual conduct of public talk in the United States. Topics addressed during these two days include:

  • Modeling Public Discourse in Popular Culture: Film critic/author Neal Gabler addresses how film stimulates and serves as a model for public discourse.

  • Cybercommunities: New Discourse for a New Century: A panel discuss the possibilities and limitations of community and public discourse in cyberspace.

  • Creating the Conversation: The Dynamics of Public Discourse:(featuring the work/techniques of Philadelphia's Interact Theater Company):

  • Making It Happen I: Improving Political Discourse in the 21st Century: Three political veterans discuss ways to improve the nature and conduct of political campaigns in America.

  • Making It Happen II: Exemplary Discourse Programs: Representatives from exemplary organizations identified by the Commission discuss their innovative and productive forms of social and political discourse.
