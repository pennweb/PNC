What is Penn Talks?
PennTalks is a program led by the Penn Public Talk Project and co-sponsored by the UA and Civic House. It is a unique opportunity for students to reflect on their experiences and to identify their hopes, dreams, and aspirations for the Penn community through a series of small group conversations in their college houses, fraternities, sororities, interest organizations, or any place that Penn students get together. Findings from the group conversations will be presented to President Judith Rodin and other Penn leaders to provide them with a better understanding of student visions of their university.


When does it start and how can I become involved?
Students are invited to participate as either facilitators or members of a group. The program starts with facilitator training on Friday, March 3, with meetings set to take place over the next few weeks. To participate, all you need to do is go to the PennTalks registration page, tell us a little about yourself, and we will get back to you with the times and places that group conversations will occur.


Why should I participate?
By being a part of PennTalks, you can share your unique thoughts and feelings on Penn -- as it is now and as it could be in the future -- directly with other students and, in distilled form, to Dr. Rodin and the rest of the Penn community. Facilitators will receive professional training that they will be able to use later in a variety of academic, workplace, and community environments. (And it wouldn't be a bad item on your resume´ either!)


What will happen in my group conversation?
A conversation features a small group of individuals learning from one another in an interactive, collaborative manner. Discussion guides will be distributed in advance to all participants. The guides will feature a variety of questions and alternate viewpoints designed to stimulate discussion. Specially trained student volunteer facilitators will lead the conversations.


What will the facilitators do?
Facilitators will lead the discussions, listen, and record the results of the group conversation. They will receive formal training on Friday, March 3. They will set-up a group of their choice. Together with a partner, they will lead two sessions over the course of the next month. At each session, one facilitator will direct the conversation and insure that everyone present has the opportunity to fully participate in the process. The other will act as note-taker, and will assist in leading the dialogue. All facilitators will reconvene as a group on Friday, April 7 to identify the major themes which emerged at their sessions. They will also report back to Dr. Rodin on April 14.


What is the Penn Public Talk Project?
The Penn Public Talk Project is a program of the Penn National Commission on Society, Culture and Community. The PNC was convened and chaired by President Rodin in December 1996 "to address the polarization, oversimplification, and isolation from important cultural and intellectual traditions that are increasingly characteristic of contemporary social and political discourse." Programs developed by the Penn Public Talk Project embody the central insights of the Commission, that the most productive public discourse integrates rational deliberation with personal narrative and relational features to strengthen communities. Such fully realized, robust discourse enables citizens of diverse backgrounds and opinions to do real work together on issues of perceived consequence and importance in their own lives and communities.
