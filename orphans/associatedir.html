ASSOCIATE DIRECTOR

PENN NATIONAL COMMISSION ON SOCIETY, CULTURE AND COMMUNITY

ASSOCIATE DIRECTOR VII (05688SH)

Plan, develop & support major programmatic aspects of national commission on social, political & cultural issues; plan, organize & support working groups focusing on issues of intolerance, leadership & community; develop research projects; commission original analysis & research; prepare expositions of the Commission's ideas; plan & implement major outreach initiatives to current & emerging leaders, professional organizations, community groups, schools & colleges; coordinate Commission research projects, recruiting & maintaining liaison with researchers; write & edit communications materials; coordinate events; supervise other project staff as assigned.

Qualifications: PhD or equivalent; 4-6 years college faculty experience in social sciences, humanities or related discipline; strong interest in & familiarity with contemporary intellectual, political, cultural & social issues, especially contemporary literature on democracy, civility, cultural conflict, identity, values, diversity, leadership & related topics; excellent interpersonal skills & demonstrated ability to interact effectively with faculty & individuals at senior academic & professional levels required; excellent oral & written communication skills.

Please note: The search will remain open until the position is filled. The anticipated starting date is October 1, 1997. This is a two-year position that will end June 30, 1999.
